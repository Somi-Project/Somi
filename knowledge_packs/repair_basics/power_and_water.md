# Field Repair: Power and Water Triage

Start by stabilizing hazards before trying to restore service.

1. Isolate fuel leaks, exposed wiring, pressure lines, and contaminated water.
2. Confirm inputs before replacing parts: fuel, air, spark, compression, suction, and clean output.
3. Test one subsystem at a time with a known-good load such as a lamp, charger, or short water draw.

Practical checks:
- Generator power: inspect battery charge, fuel quality, intake blockage, oil level, breaker position, and grounding.
- Water pumps: prime the pump, inspect inlet blockage, verify seals, and confirm the outlet is not dead-headed.
- Filtration: replace clogged prefilters first and backflush only if the filter design allows it.

If a repair is uncertain, document the symptom, what was already tested, and what changed after each adjustment. That record matters when parts and time are limited.
