# Water, Shelter, and Sanitation Basics

Clean water and sanitation protect the group faster than almost any other intervention.

Water priorities:
- Use the cleanest source available first.
- Filter visible sediment before boiling or chemical treatment.
- Keep raw-water containers separate from treated-water containers.
- Label treated water with the date and treatment method.

Shelter priorities:
- Keep sleeping areas dry, ventilated, and separated from waste handling.
- Reduce smoke exposure from cooking and heating.
- Protect stored food from insects, rodents, and standing water.

Sanitation priorities:
- Wash hands after waste handling, food prep, and wound care.
- Keep waste downhill and downwind from water and living areas when possible.
- Treat diarrhea, fever, and dehydration as immediate hygiene and water-quality signals.
