# Triage and Wound Care Basics

Start with airway, breathing, circulation, major bleeding, and level of
consciousness.

Practical steps:

- control major bleeding first
- clean wounds with the safest available clean water before dressing
- document time, symptoms, and any medicine or procedure given
- mark patients who need repeated checks for breathing, temperature, pain, or
  confusion

Escalate quickly for:

- uncontrolled bleeding
- difficulty breathing
- signs of shock
- worsening infection, spreading redness, or high fever
