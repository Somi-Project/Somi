# Sanitation and Sterilization Basics

Good sanitation protects a recovering shelter faster than most advanced
interventions.

Core priorities:

- keep clean water containers physically separate from raw water and waste
- wash hands after waste handling, wound care, and food preparation
- disinfect tools before reuse with a documented method and contact time
- move latrines, burn pits, and grey-water runoff downwind and downhill from
  living and cooking areas whenever terrain allows

Field reminders:

- label clean, dirty, and sharps zones
- boil or pressure-sterilize equipment when chemical sterilants are unavailable
- isolate symptomatic illness early and track who handled contaminated tools
